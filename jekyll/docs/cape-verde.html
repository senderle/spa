Cape Verde

Cape Verde is one of the continent's smallest countries. This archipelago consists of 10 islands off the western coast of Africa. About 500,000 people live in Cape Verde, 124,000 of which live in its capital city Praia. Originally colonized by the Portuguese, they achieved independence in 1975. The country has a long history of emigration due to the rugged terrain that makes agriculture difficult. Tourism accounts for about 18% of the country's GDP, and the country hosts around 300,000 visitors a year. Otherwise, the government relies on foreign aid and emigrants remittances.
