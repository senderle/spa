Sao Tome and Principe

Export Data

This small island country has two volcanic islands and several small islets in the Gulf of Guinea off Africa's West Coast. The country has about .2 million people, with about 62% of them living in urban spaces. The capital city is São Tomé. The Portuguese colonized the island, who used the islands for plantations where sugar, coffee, and cocoa were produced. The country achieved independence in 1975. Agriculture remains an essential aspect of the country's economy; cocoa is their main cash crop. Since independence, much of the government has been run by the Movement de Libertação de São Tomé e Príncipe; which was initially Marxist but has been recently centered on personal power struggles. Fradique de Menezes won the 2001 and 2006 elections. In 2010 a parliamentary election established Patrice Truvada as prime minister. The country elected its first president in 2011, who was replaced in 2016 by Evaristo Carvalho.
